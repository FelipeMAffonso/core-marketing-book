The loose question

“Give me a SWOT”

  • Right-side-up only, the world as it is today.
  • Returns a comfortable, flattering list.
  • Every box reads as a reason the brand is winning.
  • It has not been stress-tested against a single shift.
The Flip Test · same model, same bill

The two adversarial reads

  • Upside-down: under what shift does each strength flip into a weakness? Stanley's cultural relevance softens once the look loses its oxygen.
  • Outside-in: how does a named rival describe you, and what move does it make next? Yeti defends men's outdoor utility and prices crossover out of reach.

A free assistant runs both reads on a brand you own, so a SWOT becomes a working instrument only once you make it argue against you.

Source: Ferrell, Hartline & Hochstein, Marketing Strategy 8e (2022); the disruptive-mindset three-read overlay
