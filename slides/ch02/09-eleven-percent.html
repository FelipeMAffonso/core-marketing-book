Across fifteen categories, only 11% of a brand's own buyers see it as different from the rest.

Exhibit 2.4 · A brand's own buyers, split by why they choose it · Ehrenberg-Bass 15-category audit

The other 89% bought it because it was familiar, in stock, and thought of first. A brand competes on being easy to think of and easy to find, not on a difference buyers do not feel.

So what

The two assets that move sales are mental and physical availability.

The two market-based assets

  • Mental: easy to think of when the need shows up.
  • Physical: easy to find on the shelf or in search.
  • A buyer who can think of it but not find it does not buy.

Source: Sharp, How Brands Grow (2010); Ehrenberg-Bass 15-category audit; mean across categories, share varies by category
