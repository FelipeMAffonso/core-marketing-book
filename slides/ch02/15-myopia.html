Marketing myopia is naming yourself by the product when the buyer hired you for a job.

Exhibit 2.9 · Name the product and rivals escape the box · name the job and they become your options

The fix

Name the job the buyer is hiring you for, not the product you happen to sell.

Jobs to be done

  • A commuter does not want a drill; she wants a quarter-inch hole.
  • The railroads kept perfecting locomotives while cars took the job.
  • The same script later ran on Kodak when film gave way to digital.

Source: Levitt, “Marketing Myopia,” HBR (1960); Christensen et al., Competing Against Luck (2016)
