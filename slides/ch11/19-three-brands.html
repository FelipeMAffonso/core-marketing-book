Costco, Trader Joe's, and Stanley each run channels where every layer does one job no other layer can.

Costco · channel is the strategy $269.9B FY2025 net sales across 914 warehouses, almost no advertising. The $65 membership fee is the gate and the profit engine behind a 14% markup ceiling.
Trader Joe's · narrow is the moat ~80% Of the shelf is private label, against a 16–20% industry average. About 4,000 SKUs and no app or loyalty program, on purpose.
Stanley · the uncommissioned channel $750M 2023 Quencher revenue, up from $73M in five years. An Instagram group it never hired funded the order; seven channels now run in parallel.

When a channel a firm did not authorize is producing the inflection, the move is to feed it rather than gatekeep it, because pulling any one layer out breaks the system the others depend on.

Source: Costco FY2025 annual report; Trader Joe's case (2024–2026); Stanley/Buy Guide reporting, 2016–2024
