Walmart's price, layout, and supply chain were all right, yet the channel was wrong for Germany.

The home playbook · right

Three things Bentonville got right

The 1997 entry brought the cheapest-prices playbook into 85 hypermarkets.

  • Prices matched the Arkansas standard
  • Layouts modeled on U.S. Supercenters
  • Supply chain integrated with global procurement
The local channel · wrong

Four local conditions each broke one piece

The 200,000-square-foot system met a market built the other way.

  • Aldi and Lidl already sold staples cheaper, closer to home
  • Labor law required eight-hour shifts the schedule could not use
  • Zoning blocked the giant store near homes; the chant was banned

Source: Knorr & Arndt, Walmart-Germany case (2003); Walmart sold the 85 stores to Metro AG in 2006 after a loss above $1B
