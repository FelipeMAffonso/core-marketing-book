Push spends on the channel and pull spends on the buyer, so most brands run both and the proportions are the decision.

Push · spend on the channel

The paid retailer pushes it to the buyer

Trade promotion, slotting fees, and co-op allowances buy the shelf.

  • Slotting fees can run tens of thousands per item per chain
  • Supermarket buyers reject 70%-plus of new items offered weekly
Pull · spend on the buyer

The primed buyer asks the shelf for it

Advertising, content, social, and search build the mental availability from Chapter 6.

  • The buyer asks for the brand and the retailer stocks it to meet demand
  • Pull is the work that makes the brand come to mind at the cue

Outside the top three brands, CPG trade-marketing budgets regularly exceed advertising budgets, because the push side pays for shelf access the pull side cannot win on its own.

Source: Kotler, Keller & Chernev, Marketing Management 16e (2022); U.S. CPG trade promotion runs roughly 20% of category revenue
