The highest-leverage homework is the job the buyer is hiring the product to do, not who the buyer is.

The job statement · fixed three-clause syntax

When it's 7am and I'm driving to work, I want to keep one hand free for half an hour, so I can stay entertained until the parking lot.

So the milkshake's real rival is a banana or a bagel, not another milkshake.

How you harvest it

Run the switch interview with buyers who recently switched. About ten cover three-to-five patterns across ninety percent of a category.

Why the syntax matters

  • Strip the circumstance and it reverts to demographics.
  • "Busy professional" is not a job; "7am, driving" is.
  • The job exists before any product does.

Source: Christensen and Moesta, Jobs-to-Be-Done; the morning-milkshake case; switch-interview pattern across a 3,500-launch dataset
