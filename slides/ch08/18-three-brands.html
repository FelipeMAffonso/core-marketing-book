The same machinery runs on three live brands, and the product is never only the thing in the box.

Patagonia · service-augmented 174,799 Garments repaired in fiscal 2025 across 70 global centers. Worn Wear is inside the product boundary, so repair reads as a feature the buyer paid for.
Stanley · one line, stretched $73M→$750M Revenue, 2018 to 2023, all from a cup the team nearly discontinued. A depth stretch, a repositioning, and a drop cadence ran in parallel.
Liquid Death · the can is the product $1.4B Valuation by March 2024, inside seven years. Tap water does the functional job for less, so the buyer pays for the can and the brand voice.

Each brand's lift came from the augmented layer, not the commodity inside, so reading the product as only the water, the cup, or the jacket misses where the value actually lives.
