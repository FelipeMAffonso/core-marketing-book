Two biases compound, so a new product faces a roughly nine-times barrier before buyers switch.

Buyers overvalue what they have 3× Loss aversion plus the endowment effect. The thing already owned feels worth more than an equal new gain.
×
Sellers overvalue what they built 3× The innovator's blind spot. The team reads its product as far better than the market will.
=
The adoption barrier 9× The mismatch buyers feel. A product has to be far better, not a little better, before they move.

The two fixes the chapter's cases show: cut the loss, so the new product behaves like the old one, or aim at non-owners who have nothing to give up, which is why the AirPods hearing-aid feature was almost pure gain.

Source: Gourville, “Eager Sellers and Stony Buyers,” Harvard Business Review, June 2006 (the 9x mismatch)
