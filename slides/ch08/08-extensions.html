Most new products are extensions, and the binding question is whether the parent's meaning fits the category.

Line extension · lower risk A new variant inside a category the brand already serves.

A new Quencher colorway, Tide Pods beside Tide Liquid. Buyers already grant the brand credibility here, so the upside is bounded by what the category can absorb.

Brand extension · higher risk A new category, where the parent's meaning can dilute it.

Bic carried cheap, functional, throwaway into a $5 perfume. Buyers wanted lasting and premium, the opposite, and the line died within two years.

Run the brand-fit test before any prototype budget: name what the parent means, name what the category requires, and where they conflict the extension dilutes, because no media spend dissolves a conflict the parent already carries.

Source: Kotler, Keller & Chernev 16e (2022); line- vs. brand-extension framework, brand-dilution principle, Bic perfume failure
