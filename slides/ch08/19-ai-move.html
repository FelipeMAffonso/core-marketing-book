Hand a product to an assistant that searches the live web, and the sharp ask is the one that earns a verdict.

Vague request

“What life-cycle stage is this product in?”

Returns a stage with no evidence behind it. You already knew that much, and you cannot check it or act on it.

Sharp request

“Search the last 3 years of sales trend, saturation, and competitor count. Return the stage, the one job it hands the marketer, and the single piece of evidence that would change your answer.”

Returns a stage, a job, and what would change it: a dated hypothesis you can check, which is exactly what the curve is meant to be.

Same model, same cost, only the question changed. The lesson the whole chapter keeps returning to is that a vague brief invents a verdict and a sharp one earns it.
