Read a recovery on three justices, where the conversation outweighs the compensation amount.

The outcome

Distributive

The tangible compensation the buyer walks away with. The test is whether the amount matches the magnitude of the loss, because $5 on a $200 loss insults and $500 on a $5 loss confuses.

The process

Procedural

The speed and accessibility of the resolution path. A refund in two minutes through one click is a different experience from the same amount through four phone calls.

The conversation · weighs most

Interactional

The empathy, honesty, and owned effort of the person the buyer talks to. A smaller refund delivered with empathy leaves the buyer more loyal than a larger one delivered as a transaction.

Pulling the last twenty resolved complaints and scoring each on the three dimensions usually shows one firm-specific leak: generous but cold, warm but slow, or fast but silent.

Source: Tax, Brown & Chandrashekaran, J. of Marketing (1998); the three-justice decomposition of service recovery
