Buyers weight reliability first and tangibles last, so most firms spend most on the dimension that moves judgment least.

Exhibit 9.3 · The five SERVQUAL dimensions, ranked by weight in the buyer's overall quality judgment

RATER is the memory aid, but the acronym order is not the importance order.

So what

Reliability is the hardest to see and the easiest to cut in a budget meeting, and it is what the buyer weights first.

The inversion

  • Tangibles is the most expensive to manage and most visible.
  • It is the least important of the five to the buyer.
  • The budget aims at the dimension that moves the needle least.
