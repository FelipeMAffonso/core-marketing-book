Four characteristics separate a service from a good, and each one forces one operating discipline.

Cannot pre-inspect

Intangibility

The buyer pays for a promise, so the discipline is physical evidence.

Buyer in the process

Inseparability

The encounter is the product, so the discipline is internal marketing.

Depends on who, when, where

Variability

Quality wanders by shift, so the discipline is the service blueprint.

Cannot be inventoried

Perishability

The empty 7 a.m. seat is gone, so the discipline is yield pricing.

The traits are an engine, not a box

Each trait creates a problem the firm cannot wish away, and the field built one discipline to absorb each. The four disciplines organize the rest of the chapter.

Source: Kotler, Keller & Chernev, Marketing Management 16e (2022), p. 185; Zeithaml, Bitner & Gremler
