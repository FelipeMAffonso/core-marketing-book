The few high-attention encounters carry the brand, so SAS funded the front line ahead of the assets the buyer never sees.

A moment of truth is any short encounter where the buyer is watching closely enough to judge the brand from that moment alone. Most firms staff the front line as the cheapest delivery layer. SAS reversed the order, authorizing staff to solve problems without manager approval and following them with the training budget.

10M customers a year
× 5 employees met each
× 15s per encounter
≈ 50M fifteen-second moments of truth a year, more brand exposure than any TV budget

SAS turned a $17 million loss into a $54 million profit in the first year, because the encounter is where the promise gets kept or broken.

Source: Carlzon, Moments of Truth (1987); Normann, SAS Service Management System
