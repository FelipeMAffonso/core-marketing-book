The journey map is everything above the line of visibility; the blueprint is the same map plus the backstage.

Frontstage only

Customer-journey map

Walks the buyer's path step by step and tags the emotion at each one.

  • Awareness, booking, arrival, the core service, departure, follow-up
  • Everything on it is something the buyer sees and feels
  • Easy to draw from the buyer's side, so teams build it first
Frontstage plus backstage

Service blueprint

Keeps the frontstage row, then adds the line of visibility and everything below it.

  • The back-stage staff and support systems the buyer never sees
  • A frontstage emotion dip usually has a backstage cause
  • The line of visibility is the seam between the two diagrams

Source: Shostack (1984); the journey-map-to-blueprint conversion, Zeithaml, Bitner & Gremler
