Buyers resist the AI pick in identity-laden categories, except when the inventory is vanishing.

Default · identity-laden categories she picks the human

Asked to choose between a human advisor and an algorithm, a buyer picks the human, and the preference sharpens after one visible mistake. Clothing, gifts, books, and music trigger it, because the things she owns extend her sense of self.

The reversal · vanishing inventory she takes the agent

When stock is running out, the buyer welcomes the higher-autonomy agent. The question shifts from whether its taste is good enough to whether it will get the option before it is gone. Stanley limited drops sell out in about four minutes.

The aversion is an attention state, not a fixed trait, and it inverts again when the human reads as corruptible. A brand should rank category-context cells by inventory volatility and ship the higher-autonomy flow in the top quintile.

Source: Dietvorst, Simmons & Massey, JEP: General (2015); Frank et al., Psychology & Marketing (2026); Belk, JCR (1988)
