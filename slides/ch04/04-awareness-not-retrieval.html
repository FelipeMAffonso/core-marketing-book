A buyer can recognize a brand on a list and still not produce it in the moment, so awareness is not retrieval.

Exhibit 4.2 · Where 143 weeknight TV ads leaked · share surviving each step

Of every 100 ads aired, about 40 were recognized after broadcast, and only 40% of those were tied to the right brand. The noticed-and-correctly-branded share lands near 16.

What the funnel costs

Five-sixths of the exposure never reaches a branded memory at all.

The implication for spend

  • Recognition surveys count the 40%, not the 16%, and overstate the work an ad did.
  • Small exposures across many cues beat one blockbuster that hits a buyer once.
  • The Super Bowl spot ties the brand to a couch the buyer will not sit on at the shelf.

Source: Australian study of 143 weeknight TV ads, in Sharp, How Brands Grow (Ehrenberg-Bass, 2010); figures rounded
