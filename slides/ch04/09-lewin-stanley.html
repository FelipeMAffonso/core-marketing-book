Stanley's buyer ran B = f(P, E), and the cup went from $73M to $750M because the environment moved, not the buyer.

Person (P) · held constant unchanged

motives, identity, attitudes

The young professional's motives in 2024 were about what they were in 2019. A brand that models only segments and personas reads only this side of the equation.

Environment (E) · where it moved $73M → $750M

cues, alternatives, social context

TikTok gave the cup social meaning, color-drops turned it into a gift, and its presence at offices and gyms built a field tension the 2019 environment did not hold.

Nothing about the product changed across those five years. The environment side is where most of the change happens, because that side is the cue layer the buyer's autopilot actually runs on.

Source: Lewin (1936); Kowitt, CNBC (Dec 23, 2023); Stanley cup-line revenue $73M (2018) to $750M (2023)
