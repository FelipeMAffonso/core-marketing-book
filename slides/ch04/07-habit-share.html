Roughly 43% of daily action repeats in the same context while the buyer is thinking about something else.

Daily behavior · habitual 43% Of daily action is repeated in context. Experience-sampling diaries return the figure across age groups and categories since the 1990s.
Consumer purchases · impulse 27–62% Estimated impulse-buy range. Even the floor of that range undercuts the image of the careful deliberator.
After a life event · re-deliberation 4–12 wks The habit discontinuity window. A move or a new job dissolves the old cues, and a promotion inside the window produces structural lift.

A reasons-based campaign aimed at a habitual buyer in a stable context produces no measurable lift, because she is not listening to the argument. She is answering the cue, and the cue layer is the only point a brand can cheaply enter or block.

Source: Quinn et al., PSPB (2010); Wood, Good Habits, Bad Habits (2019); Hoyer, MacInnis & Pieters 7e (2018)
