A brand as a memory network · the central name plus its learned cues

A brand is a network of memory connections between a name and the cues tied to it through repeated exposure.

Touch any one node and the activation spreads outward to the rest. That is why a strong brand survives a packaging change or a crisis.

A brand built on a single node collapses the moment that node is touched.

Source: Hoyer, MacInnis & Pieters, Consumer Behavior 7e (2018); spreading-activation model
