A small brand has fewer buyers who also buy less often. The gap from seven to nine is far too small to rescue it.

Exhibit 7.7 · Double jeopardy · penetration by purchase frequency, twelve brands

The two penalties

Coca-Cola: ~75% of cola buyers, ~9 times a year. Dr Pepper: ~25%, ~7 times.

The only way off the line

  • Up and to the right at once: win more buyers.
  • Higher frequency then follows from size.
  • Retention work cannot buy a small brand high frequency.
