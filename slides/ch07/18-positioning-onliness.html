The four-blank positioning statement

For [this target], the brand is the only [frame of reference] that [value claim], because of [reasons to believe].

Fill the blanks with the three Cs: what the consumer wants, what the competition already owns, and what the company can truthfully back.

Parity vs difference

Points of parity are the category must-haves: clean, cold, safe. Skip one and the brand is cut before its difference is heard. The point of difference is the one ownable thing that wins the choice.

The swap test

Drop a competitor's name into your sentence. A claim that still reads true with a rival's name in it is generic and has to be tightened until only your brand fits.
