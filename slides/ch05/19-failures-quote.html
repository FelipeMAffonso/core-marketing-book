The shape every research failure shares · Chapter 5, §5.7

Six failures share it. Selection bias asks the wrong people. Expectancy bias signals the answer, fixed by a double-blind. The observer effect changes behavior once watched. A leading question smuggles the answer in. Non-response bias keeps the people who declined, and in 2026 about 10 to 25 percent of a raw panel is bots. Stopping early grabs a fluke, because noise is loudest in small samples. Each has a plain fix a marketer can name.
