Coding 25 transcripts used to cost three analyst days. A model now does it in five minutes for cents.

Manual pass · 2024 $1,500–3,000 An analyst's three-day read of 25 interview transcripts, tagged and rolled into themes by hand.
Model pass · 2026 ~$0.30–1 The same read in about five minutes, with prompt caching on, leaving you the two parts it cannot do.
Accuracy · benchmarked 93.9% Agreement with a trained human coder on the British Election Study's 50-category open-text item.

The two parts the model cannot do for you are writing the rubric that says what counts as a theme, and checking that the model followed it. Lean too hard and the output drifts toward common phrasings, flattening the rare themes that are often the most interesting.

Source: Gilardi et al., PNAS (2023); Mellon et al. (2024) on the BES item; Anthropic API pricing, 2026
