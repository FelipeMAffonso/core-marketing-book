The right research spend is the cost of being wrong: sometimes large, sometimes a side door, sometimes zero.

Stanley · under-spent ~$700M Left on the table, 2019–2023, on $0 of research. Twenty interviews for about $8,000 would have found the women buyers.
Patagonia · side door ~92,000 Worn Wear repairs a year in North America, each a ticket on its heaviest users, paid out of the repair budget.
Trader Joe's · on purpose $0 No loyalty program, app, or account. Fast rotation and aisle staff read the market, and it ranks near the top of dunnhumby 2025.

All three sit in different cells of the calibration map, and all three are doing the same arithmetic correctly. What drives the budget is the cost of being wrong on the decision in front of you, never last year's number.
