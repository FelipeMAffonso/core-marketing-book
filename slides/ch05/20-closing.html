Scope a study before you spend a dollar on it, on a brand you actually know.

01 Write the two problems apart. The decision (ship A or B) on one line, the research question (what share picks each, which feature drives it) under it. Collapsing them is what New Coke got wrong.
02 Put a number on being wrong. Cost of the wrong call, times the chance the lean is wrong, is the EVPI. A single study should fall at 2 to 10 percent of it.
03 Pick the mode. Words about reasoning go qualitative, numbers about how-many go quantitative, records of behavior go observational. If you cannot write a good survey yet, qualitative first.
04 Name the population, then the trap. Who the decision is about, and the convenience sample you would reach for if lazy. That gap is the selection bias that cost Stanley its women buyers.
