Buyers differ, so a seller who ignores the differences serves a customer who exists nowhere.

Two people reach for the same forty-ounce cup. One drinks water on a worksite, and one likes that the color matches her car. That variation is the structure of the market, not noise around some typical buyer. The formal name for it is customer heterogeneity.

  • The mind holds room for three to seven brands per category, often closer to three
  • A brand with no clear claim never makes that short list
  • The everybody trap: mean something to everyone, mean something specific to no one
  • The cure is to pick a target and accept the buyers who wanted something else

Source: Ries & Trout (2001); Smith, J. of Marketing (1956); Palmatier & Sridhar (2021), Ch. 2
