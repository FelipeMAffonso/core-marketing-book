Four bases describe a segment, but the benefit a buyer wants is the cause of the choice.

Where the buyer is

Geographic

Country to ZIP to block. Tells you how to reach a buyer, not which one.

Who the buyer is

Demographic

Age, income, life stage. The most legible base, but it says who, not why.

What the buyer values

Psychographic

Attitudes and lifestyle. Sorts a retiree and a young climber together.

What the buyer does

Behavioral

Usage, occasion, loyalty. The most predictive, because it reads behavior.

Underneath all four · benefit segmentation

A 1968 study cut toothpaste into Worriers, Sociables, Sensories, and Independents: same age and income, four different brand choices.

Source: Haley, J. of Marketing (1968); Kotler, Keller & Chernev, Marketing Management 16e (2022), Ch. 6
