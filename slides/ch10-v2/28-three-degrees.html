Three degrees

How finely the seller can sort buyers before the price reaches them.

First degree

One price per buyer

A unique price for each transaction, set close to each buyer's WTP.

car-dealer haggle, art-gallery quote

Second degree

By self-selected quantity

Buyers sort themselves by what they choose to buy, and per-unit price changes with the choice.

bulk discounts, two-part tariffs

Third degree

By observable segment

Seller posts different prices to groups defined by a signal: age, geography, channel, time.

student tickets, off-peak rates, geo-priced SaaS

Source: Pigou, The Economics of Welfare (1920), Part II, Ch. 17, §3.
