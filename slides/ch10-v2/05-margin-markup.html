Margin and markup

Three dollars of profit yield two different ratios, because the two formulas use different denominators.

One sale · $7 cost + $3 profit = $10 revenue

Why the gap

  • Margin reads against revenue
  • Markup reads against cost
  • Same dollars, two denominators

Where it matters

  • Channel deals quote markup
  • P&L statements report margin
  • Confusion misstates channel profitability

Source: cost-accounting convention; Marn & Rosiello, HBR (1992) on the channel-margin error.
