Cost-plus

Price is set as unit cost plus a chosen markup, anchored on the cost floor of the value stick.

Food-truck burrito · markup of 40 percent on unit cost

When it fits

  • WTP cannot be measured reliably
  • Unit-cost data is well-defined
  • Buyers do not compare alternatives

Where it fails

  • WTP exceeds the cost-plus price
  • Differentiation goes uncaptured
  • A competitor captures the surplus

Source: Marn & Rosiello, HBR (1992); standard cost-accounting formulation.
