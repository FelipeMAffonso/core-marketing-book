Charm pricing

The buyer encodes the leftmost digit as the price signal before fully parsing the trailing digits.

Anderson & Simester 1996 catalog experiment · same dress, three prices

The mechanism

  • Reading scans left to right
  • The leftmost digit anchors price magnitude
  • Trailing digits register only after

Where it works

  • Catalog and supermarket retail
  • Just-below thresholds ($9, $19, $99)
  • Goods buyers scan rather than evaluate

Source: Anderson & Simester, Quantitative Marketing and Economics (1996), catalog field experiment.
