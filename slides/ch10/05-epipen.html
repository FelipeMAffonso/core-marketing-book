The EpiPen climbed from $100 to $608 over nine years and broke only when the fairness ceiling caught up.

Exhibit 10.3 · EpiPen two-pack wholesale price · 2007 to 2016, US dollars

Each of roughly fifteen steps sat below the level a buyer notices, so the climb drew no reaction for nine years. The 2016 Senate hearing made the cumulative line visible, and the dual-entitlement story no longer held.

The two-question test

Whether a firm can raise price lives in cost and power; whether it should lives in fairness.

What broke it

  • The vulnerable group was parents of allergic children.
  • The product was a life-saving necessity.
  • Within a year Mylan committed to a half-price generic.
