The same dollar increase reads as fair after a cost rise and as gouging after a demand spike.
Dual-entitlement principle · Kahneman, Knetsch & Thaler, AER (1986)

Buyers grant the firm a reference profit and the right to defend it against rising costs, but not a windfall captured from a demand shift. A 2024 study sharpens the rollout rule: buyers rated an algorithm-set price gap as more fair than the identical human-set gap, because a human could have chosen otherwise and an algorithm reads as a rule-follower. Profit-driven personalized pricing belongs behind a published-rule algorithm, while a senior or hardship rate keeps a human face.

Source: Kahneman, Knetsch & Thaler, AER (1986); Duani, Barasch & Morwitz, JACR (2024)
