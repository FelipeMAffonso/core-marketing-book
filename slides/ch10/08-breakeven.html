A $5 drink that costs $1.50 breaks even at 2,000 cups, because the contribution margin sets the target, not the sticker.

Exhibit 10.5 · Breakeven for a campus drink · $5 price, $1.50 variable cost, $7,000 fixed cost

Move the price, move the target

A wider margin pulls breakeven left; a thinner one pushes it right.

The same drink, three prices

  • $6 price → $4.50 margin → about 1,556 cups.
  • $5 price → $3.50 margin → 2,000 cups.
  • $4 price → $2.50 margin → 2,800 cups.

Source: Kotler, Keller & Chernev, Marketing Management 16e (2022); illustrative campus-drink figures
