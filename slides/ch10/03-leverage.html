At a 3% margin, a 1% price move lifts profit about 33%, while a 1% volume move lifts it only about 10%.

Exhibit 10.1 · Profit lift from a 1% move at a 3% net margin · $100 revenue, $97 cost

Raise price 1% at flat cost and profit jumps from $3 to $4. Matching that one dollar through volume takes about 33% more units, because each extra unit drags its share of cost.

Why the gap is so wide

Extra price carries no extra cost, so it falls straight to profit.

The long-run example

  • Netflix climbed $7.99 to $15.99 a month from 2010 to 2024.
  • One more streamed minute costs near zero, so each move passed through.
  • A grocer raising margarine a dollar still buys and ships the next tub.

Source: Marn & Rosiello, HBR (1992); Simon-Kucher engagements; figures illustrate the 3% net-margin case
