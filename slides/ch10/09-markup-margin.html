A $60 cost sold at $90 is a 50% markup but only a 33% margin, so the same dollars read two ways.

Markup · base is cost 50%

($90 − $60) ÷ $60

Measures the $30 added against what the unit cost. The base is smaller, so markup can run past 100%.

Margin · base is price 33%

($90 − $60) ÷ $90

Measures the same $30 against the price. The base always includes the cost, so margin never reaches 100%.

Mixing the two is how a team prices for a healthy-sounding markup and then finds the margin too thin to cover the fixed costs the breakeven chart just made visible.

Source: Kotler, Keller & Chernev, Marketing Management 16e (2022), pricing chapter
