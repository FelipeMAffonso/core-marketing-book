Audiences read charts, not tables. Your job is to make the answer visible.

A finding exists, practically, once your audience sees it in under ten seconds. Everything else on the slide (axes, gridlines, borders, legends) should earn its place or get deleted.

  • Delete 3-D, heavy gridlines, borders, legends
  • Every pixel should encode data, or be cut
  • Whitespace is structure, not emptiness
  • If the title isn't a sentence, the slide isn't ready

Source: Tufte, Visual Display of Quantitative Information (1983) · Knaflic (2015)
