A 10% price cut bought only 3% more volume, while a 10% raise lifted profit 15%. EDLP is a margin sacrifice.

Exhibit 12.8 · Dominick's Finer Foods field experiment · response to a symmetric 10% price move

A controlled test across twenty-six categories: the EDLP cut gave up thirteen cents of margin for three cents of volume, a losing trade.

Pay for it elsewhere

EDLP repays its sacrifice only where one of three offsets covers it.

The three offsets

  • Scale-driven supplier leverage (Walmart, Home Depot)
  • SKU concentration plus private label (Costco, Aldi)
  • A second revenue stream (membership fees, retail media)

Source: Hoch, Drèze & Purk, "EDLP, Hi-Lo, and Margin Arithmetic," J. of Marketing 58(4), 1994; Dominick's scanner data, 26 categories
