EDLP and HiLo train two different shopper habits. JCPenney broke the wrong one and lost about $1 billion.

EDLP · everyday low price

Trust today's price

A constant low price with little promotional cycling, so the shopper stops comparison-shopping.

  • Walmart, Costco, and Aldi run it
  • The Costco rotisserie is $4.99 because it always is
  • A margin sacrifice that needs a structural offset
HiLo · high-low promotions

Wait for the deal

Higher day-to-day prices with frequent promotions, training the shopper to read the circular and time the trip.

  • Most US supermarkets and department stores run it
  • JCPenney's shoppers were attached to the coupon
  • Removing it removed the trigger that pulled them in

A pricing format is an attachment the shopper builds to an interaction pattern, so changing the format breaks the attachment, which is what the 2012 switch from HiLo to EDLP did before the chain reversed.

Source: Kotler, Keller & Chernev, Marketing Management 16e (2022), Ch. 16; JCPenney (2012)
