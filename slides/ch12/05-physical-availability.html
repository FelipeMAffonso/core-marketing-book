A brand's share bends upward with distribution, so the last, least-contested points pay most.

Exhibit 12.2 · Distribution-to-share · the curve bends, it does not run straight

Each added point of distribution returns more share than the one before it, because the last points put the brand where fewer rivals stand beside it.

The other seat

The eight decisions are the retailer's. The brand on the shelf reads physical availability, the supply-side twin of mental availability.

Two consequences

  • Necessary but not sufficient: a box she cannot recall sits unsold on a full shelf.
  • Compete on breadth and depth, in space and in time.

Source: Sharp, How Brands Grow (2010), physical availability and the convex distribution-share relationship; Ehrenberg-Bass Institute
