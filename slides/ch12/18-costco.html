Costco's membership gate is the marketing model. All eight decisions resolve to one promise.

Costco runs 147.2 million cardholders at a North American renewal rate near 92 percent, and roughly $5 billion of operating profit comes from membership fees alone. The fee is the engine. The thin eleven-percent merchandise margin is the cost the engine pays to pull buyers through the door, which is why the chain spends close to nothing on advertising. Subtract any one of the eight and the rest lose leverage, because the chain only works when they all point the same way.

  • Target market: the fee-paying member
  • Assortment: ~4,000 SKUs, 30% Kirkland
  • Services: thin in aisle, rich at the gate
  • Atmosphere: deliberately warehouse
  • Pricing: EDLP, $4.99 rotisserie loss leader
  • Incentives: the renewal itself
  • Communications: demos, near-zero spend
  • Channels: warehouses, small site, Instacart

Source: Costco Q2 FY2026 results; Fortune (Kirkland ~$86B); Light & Kiddon on differentiated loyalty
