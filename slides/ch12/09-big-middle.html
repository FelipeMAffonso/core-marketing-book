The wheel predicts a low-cost entrant must trade up. Aldi scaled the format instead.

The wheel's forecast

Enter, trade up, decline

A successful entrant adds facilities and prices, lifting its cost base until the next entrant attacks.

  • Walmart doubled its store size across the 1990s
  • Department stores reached vulnerability in the 2000s
  • Macy's closed a quarter of its footprint in 2024
The Big Middle · the counter-example

Scale the format, do not trade up

Walmart, Target, and Costco hold low price, broad assortment, and acceptable quality as a stable position.

  • Aldi at roughly 2,677 US stores by May 2026, still no trade-up
  • Each new store spreads the network across more square feet
  • Per-unit cost keeps falling as the chain grows

Source: Levy, Grewal, Peterson & Connolly, "The Big Middle," J. of Retailing 81(2), 2005; Aldi US (Jan. 2026); ScrapeHero (May 2026)
