A grocer's newest pricing decision is the ad inventory it sells, because it runs near 70% gross margin.

Walmart Connect $6.4B 2026 ad revenue, from $0 in 2018. Growth runs nearly five times the chain's e-commerce rate.
Amazon Ads $56B 2024 ad revenue. The third-largest digital-ad business, behind only Google and Meta.
The margin gap ~70% Gross margin on ad inventory, vs ~3% on merchandise. That high-margin line cross-subsidizes a shelf price a rival without one cannot match.

The cross-subsidy works only where the retailer holds a deep first-party customer file, which is why Kroger runs one across roughly sixty million households while Aldi, carrying no loyalty card, cannot.

Source: ALMCorp / Statista, Walmart Connect (Apr. 2026); eMarketer retail-media forecast (~$233.9B by 2027); analyst margin estimates
