Chapter 18 Digital Marketing and SEO/GEO — Test Bank

Chapter: 18 — Digital Marketing and SEO/GEO

Learning Objectives (Ch18, Our Chapter)

  • LO 18.1 Explain why search split into two channels in 2024 and what each channel rewards, using the dual-funnel model.
  • LO 18.2 Describe the five stacked layers of classical SEO, apply E-E-A-T signals, and explain the 2024 Helpful Content reset.
  • LO 18.3 Define generative engine optimization (GEO), name the six citation-share levers, and explain the role of RAG in AI-engine retrieval.
  • LO 18.4 Distinguish the three attribution methods (geo-incrementality, MMM, MTA), locate a team on Chaffey's maturity ladder, and explain the eBay branded-search finding.
  • LO 18.5 Explain how the cookie reversal, ATT, and the shift to first-party data reshaped the paid/owned/earned media mix.
  • LO 18.6 Describe the Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP), ChatGPT Instant Checkout, and the Product Feed Spec, and connect each to the GEO work the chapter teaches.
  • LO 18.7 Generate and evaluate FAQPage JSON-LD markup as a deployable GEO asset.
  • LO 18.8 Diagnose the five failure modes in a 2026 digital-marketing program.

Section I — Multiple-Choice Items (22 items)

Item 1

A brand holds the number two organic position on every keyword that matters, yet its organic clicks fell 31 percent year over year. The brand's pages had not changed. According to our chapter, the most likely explanation is _______.

A) competitors purchased more paid-search ads and pushed the brand's organic position below the fold B) the brand's title tags became misaligned with the query intent, reducing click-through rates C) roughly a quarter of those queries now show an AI Overview above the blue links, and buyers read the synthesized paragraph and leave without clicking D) Google's algorithm penalized the brand's backlink profile through a core update E) the brand's primary keywords shifted to lower purchase-intent long-tail variants

Answer: C

Difficulty: 1

Objective: LO 18.1 — The chapter opens with exactly this scenario: the analyst still ranks number two, but 25.11 percent of queries show an AI Overview, and Pew Research found a 3-percentage-point CTR drop (8 percent with an Overview versus 15 percent without), explaining the 31 percent traffic loss without any change to the brand's pages.

Tags: dual-funnel; AI Overview; organic CTR; AACSB: Reflective thinking

Item 2

The Brandlight AI Search Visibility Benchmark reported that the share of Google top-ten brands also appearing in AI-engine answers on the same queries fell from roughly 70 percent in 2023 to under 20 percent by early 2026. The primary implication for a marketing team is _______.

A) it should shift its entire budget to paid search before AI engines replace Google entirely B) classical SEO and generative engine optimization are two separate jobs, requiring different assets and separate measurement C) domain authority scores have lost predictive value and should be removed from dashboards D) long-tail keyword rankings are now the only viable path to organic traffic E) Google Ads became 50 percent more cost-efficient as organic competition declined

Answer: B

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.1 — The stat shows the overlap collapsed, meaning winning Google no longer predicts winning the AI citation. The chapter's response is to treat the two channels as separate jobs with different optimization units (the ranked page vs. the extractable paragraph).

Tags: dual-funnel; overlap collapse; AACSB: Application of knowledge

Item 3

A page whose product name, price, and comparison facts appear only after the page's JavaScript has run is called a _____ page, and most AI retrievers in 2026 treat it as _____.

A) dynamic-content page; high priority for retrieval because the rendered version is richer B) server-side-rendered page; acceptable because modern AI bots execute JavaScript reliably C) client-rendered page; effectively invisible because roughly 69 percent of AI crawlers cannot execute JavaScript D) progressive-enhancement page; penalized for hiding content behind interaction barriers E) JavaScript-first page; indexed as normal by Googlebot during its first-pass crawl

Answer: C

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.2 — The chapter cites the 2026 AI-crawler survey showing roughly 69 percent of named AI crawlers cannot run JavaScript, including ClaudeBot and PerplexityBot. A client-rendered page is invisible to most of the AI retrieval layer however strong its schema or backlinks.

Tags: client-rendered; JavaScript; AI crawlers; reachability; AACSB: Analytical thinking

Item 4

Google's Helpful Content System, folded into the core algorithm in March 2024, changed the SEO landscape by _______.

A) rewarding pages for keyword density above a set threshold B) elevating all pages that used structured data regardless of prose quality C) demoting pages written primarily to rank in search engines rather than to help readers, cutting low-quality content visibility by roughly 45 percent D) removing the influence of backlinks entirely in favor of on-page signals E) requiring a minimum of 2,000 words per page to qualify for top-ten placement

Answer: C

Difficulty: 1

Objective: LO 18.2 — The chapter identifies March 2024 as the reset that ended the volume-templating playbook and shifted advantage to product-led, expert-authored content the algorithm reads as written for the reader rather than the engine.

Tags: Helpful Content System; content quality; SEO reset; AACSB: Reflective thinking

Item 5

A brand manager wants to improve her brand's classical SEO ranking using product-led SEO. Which of these best describes that approach?

A) Publishing 50 to 200 templated articles per month, each targeting one long-tail keyword, to dominate the category through volume B) Each indexable page is a product unit the brand uniquely can build, such as an API method or a commerce guide, that a competitor cannot replicate without the same underlying data C) Purchasing backlinks from high-domain-authority sites to pass authority to the brand's product pages D) Optimizing every page title and meta description to maximize click-through rate from the results page E) Adding keyword-rich FAQs to every product page to capture long-tail query traffic at low production cost

Answer: B

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.2 — Product-led SEO, the Stripe/Shopify pattern, builds pages that are product units themselves rather than articles. The page exists only because the product can answer the query better than a competitor could without the same data, and the organic curve grows with the product roadmap rather than a publishing calendar.

Tags: product-led SEO; Stripe; content quality; AACSB: Application of knowledge

Item 6

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) is the mechanism AI engines use to build synthesized answers. For a brand seeking GEO citation, the most important implication of RAG is that the engine _______.

A) searches the entire public internet and weights each page by its domain authority score, identical to classical SEO B) reads only the brand's self-reported schema.org markup and ignores the prose surrounding it C) pulls a small set of relevant passages from a live index and writes its reply out of what it pulled, so the brand must shape individual extractable paragraphs rather than just optimizing the page as a whole D) generates answers entirely from its pre-training data and never retrieves current sources E) prefers paid placements from brands that advertise on the platform

Answer: C

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.3 — RAG decouples the optimization unit from the whole page. The engine retrieves passages, not pages, so the brand composes each 130-to-170-word block to stand on its own and answer one likely sub-query, and it shapes the passage to travel into a synthesized reply with attribution intact.

Tags: RAG; extractable paragraph; GEO mechanism; AACSB: Reflective thinking

Item 7

The implication for a brand whose marketing strategy ran entirely on its own blog is _______.

A) the brand is well-positioned because the engine reads owned content as the authoritative source for brand claims B) the brand's blog is likely nearly invisible to AI retrievers, which prefer the structured corpora of third-party platforms C) the brand should pivot immediately to buying banner ads on Reddit and Wikipedia D) the finding is irrelevant because Google's AI Overview weights brand-owned content equally to third-party sources E) blogs outperform Reddit only for B2B categories, so the brand's situation depends on its industry

Answer: B

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.3 — The chapter states a brand whose strategy runs on its own blog "competes in a pool its blog is not in" because 94 percent of AI citations come from earned media and only 6 percent from owned blogs. Shifting budget toward earned placement on the domains the engines prefer is the chapter's prescription.

Tags: citation concentration; earned media; GEO; AACSB: Application of knowledge

Item 8

A brand wants to improve its citation share in generative AI engines. A 10,000-query benchmark (Aggarwal et al., KDD 2024) found which single GEO lever produced the largest lift, measuring a 41 percent increase in citation frequency?

A) Publishing more pages per month to build topical authority through volume B) Adding paid search ads to signal relevance to the AI engine's retrieval index C) Inline expert quotation, where a sourced statement carries a named authority's credentials D) Increasing the number of outbound links on each page to demonstrate comprehensiveness E) Shortening the page's word count to reduce retrieval noise

Answer: C

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.3 — The chapter cites the Aggarwal GEO benchmark (10,000 queries) directly: inline expert quotation produced a 41 percent citation lift, the largest of nine levers tested. The engine reads a sourced, named quote as an authority signal.

Tags: GEO levers; expert quotation; citation share; academic benchmark; AACSB: Analytical thinking

Item 9

A brand wraps its top buyer sub-queries in FAQPage JSON-LD markup, putting the direct answer in the first 80 words of each response. Which of the following explains why this format specifically benefits GEO performance?

A) FAQPage markup increases the page's domain authority score by signaling topical depth to Google's link-valuation system B) The answer is already formatted as a response to a question, so the AI engine's lift step can quote it without reshaping the prose, and buyers satisfice on the first plausible answer they encounter C) JSON-LD markup signals paid partnership status to ChatGPT and Perplexity, which prioritize sponsored content D) The format reduces the page's HTML file size, which improves load time and classical SEO ranking E) FAQPage schema is used exclusively for e-commerce product pages and does not apply to content marketing

Answer: B

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.3 / LO 18.7 — The chapter explains FAQPage schema as GEO's highest-leverage markup because the engine's synthesis step wants to lift a passage shaped like an answer; FAQPage pre-shapes it. Satisficing means the buyer takes the first plausible answer, so being that answer in the first 80 words compounds the advantage.

Tags: FAQPage schema; JSON-LD; satisficing; GEO levers; AACSB: Application of knowledge

Item 10

The position-1/2/3 CTR curve on a classical Google results page shows roughly 40 percent, 18 percent, and 10 percent click-through rates respectively. When an AI Overview appears above those links, it functions most like _______ from the click-budget perspective.

A) a new position 0 that absorbs roughly the same click share position 1 used to capture, because the buyer reads the synthesized paragraph and stops B) a paid ad placement that supplements organic clicks without cannibalizing them C) an image carousel that increases time on the results page without affecting click distribution D) a featured snippet that redirects buyers to position 4 and below E) a penalty signal that reduces the ranking of position 1 to position 2

Answer: A

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.1 — The chapter frames the AI Overview as occupying what position 1 used to: Pew Research found 8 percent CTR with an Overview versus 15 percent without, so roughly half the position-1 clicks are absorbed into the Overview itself rather than passing to any blue link.

Tags: position CTR; AI Overview; zero-click; AACSB: Analytical thinking

Item 11

eBay ran a large-scale field experiment by turning its own paid search on and off across different markets. The study's key finding regarding branded-keyword ads was _______.

A) branded-keyword ads produced roughly two times the return on investment of non-brand keyword ads B) the ads had no measurable short-term benefit because buyers who clicked the paid brand ad would have clicked the free organic result immediately below it anyway C) Google Ads inflated eBay's revenue by roughly 20 percent by prioritizing brand searches ahead of organic listings D) the experiment confirmed that last-click attribution accurately captures branded search incrementality E) branded-keyword ads generated higher CTR but lower conversion rates than non-branded terms

Answer: B

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.4 — The eBay experiment (Blake, Nosko, Tadelis, Econometrica 2015) found branded-keyword ads were largely non-incremental: most buyers who clicked the paid brand ad would have reached the brand through the organic result anyway, so the spend mostly bought clicks the brand already had. The honest reading is that incrementality varies by query, which is why holdout experiments are needed.

Tags: eBay experiment; branded-keyword ads; incrementality; last-click; AACSB: Analytical thinking

Item 12

A marketing director reads her ad platform's last-click dashboard and moves budget away from podcast sponsorships toward branded paid search, because the paid-search column shows a strong return and podcasts show near zero. According to the chapter, the likely consequence of this decision is _______.

A) higher short-term revenue because paid search converts more reliably than audio advertising B) no change, because last-click attribution is equally accurate across all channel types C) budget drifts toward channels that catch demand and away from channels that create it, so the top of the funnel empties and revenue eventually flattens D) improved marketing-mix modeling output because the channel mix becomes simpler E) the firm benefits because podcast advertising is an unscalable vanity channel

Answer: C

Difficulty: 3

Objective: LO 18.4 — Last-click credits the final touch, which is almost always a bottom-funnel paid click. Channels like podcasts that create awareness score near zero. The chapter explains this as the mechanism behind failure mode 2: the dashboard credits the search click and quietly defunds the channel that caused the buyer to search in the first place.

Tags: last-click attribution; demand creation; budget drift; AACSB: Application of knowledge

Item 16

Section II — Scenario / Short-Answer Items (4 items)

SA-1

A regional outdoor-gear retailer sells products through its own website and ranks in Google positions 2 and 3 on queries like "best waterproof hiking boots under $150." In January 2026, the analytics team reports that the search-driven revenue from those queries has declined 28 percent year over year even though rankings have not changed. The marketing director proposes increasing the paid search budget to compensate. A digital-marketing specialist says the real problem is structural, not a budget shortfall. Using the dual-funnel model and the position CTR curve from our chapter, explain why the director's proposal may not work, and identify the two investments that would address both channels.

Sample answer: The position CTR curve gives position 2 roughly 18 percent of clicks on a clean results page. When an AI Overview appears above those links, Pew Research found the CTR drops from roughly 15 percent on Overview-free pages to 8 percent, because the buyer reads the synthesized answer and does not scroll to the organic links. Adding paid-search spend captures the same bottom-funnel buyers but does nothing to win a citation inside the AI engine's synthesized answer, so the brand appears on only one of the two surfaces the buyer now queries. The two complementary investments are: (1) classical SEO reachability checks on the product-page content so the pages serve plain HTML any AI crawler can read, including noindex-ing thin category stubs that drag down domain quality; and (2) a generative-engine optimization program that seeds a FAQPage schema on the brand's top sub-queries, a genuine participation thread in the relevant Reddit communities (r/hiking, r/ultralight), and a named-expert FAQ answer in the first 80 words of each product guide so the engine's retrieval step can lift a passage into the synthesized answer with the brand cited. These address the citation channel the paid budget cannot reach.

Difficulty: 3

Objective: LO 18.1, LO 18.2, LO 18.3

Tags: dual-funnel; CTR curve; GEO; classical SEO; AACSB: Application of knowledge; Analytical thinking

SA-2

A direct-to-consumer candle brand runs its entire digital program on Meta paid social, Google retargeting, and an owned Instagram account. Its attribution is set to last-click on both platforms. The CMO says the paid channels are delivering a blended 4x ROAS based on the dashboards. A board member asks: "Is that number real?" Using the chapter's three attribution methods and the eBay branded-search experiment, explain what would need to be true for the 4x ROAS to be accurate, and describe one test the brand could run to verify whether it is.

Sample answer: For the 4x ROAS to be real, the paid clicks would need to be the actual cause of each sale rather than merely correlated with purchases the buyer was going to make anyway. Last-click attribution assumes causation from correlation: it assigns all the credit to the final touch before purchase, which is almost always a retargeting or branded-search click. The eBay experiment directly challenged this assumption. When eBay turned its branded paid search off in some markets, it found that most of those buyers still purchased through the organic result directly below the paid ad, so the branded-keyword ads produced almost no incremental revenue despite looking strong on the last-click dashboard. For the candle brand, a 4x ROAS on last-click could mean the paid ads are truly driving sales the brand would not have otherwise made, or it could mean Meta and Google are each claiming credit for sales the buyer had already decided to make after seeing an Instagram organic post or reading a Reddit review. The test is a geo-incrementality experiment: turn off paid campaigns in one geographic market for two or three weeks while keeping them on in a similar market, then compare revenue trends. If the holdout market shows very little revenue decline, the ads are largely non-incremental and the 4x ROAS is a fiction the last-click model produced.

Difficulty: 3

Objective: LO 18.4

Tags: last-click; incrementality; eBay experiment; ROAS; AACSB: Reflective thinking; Analytical thinking

SA-3

A student graduating in May 2026 wants her portfolio to show up when a recruiter asks Claude or Perplexity "who are the best UX designers graduating from Oklahoma State this spring?" She currently has a portfolio site built on a React single-page application (SPA). Identify the two highest-leverage structural changes she should make to the site, using the chapter's GEO and classical SEO frameworks. For each change, explain the mechanism that makes it effective.

Sample answer: The two highest-leverage changes are: (1) Serve the portfolio's core content (project titles, case study text, credentials, graduation year) in the initial HTML the server delivers, not hidden behind React's JavaScript rendering. The mechanism: roughly 69 percent of AI crawlers in 2026 cannot execute JavaScript, so a React SPA that delivers a near-empty shell and populates content via JavaScript is invisible to ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and most other AI retrievers, regardless of how strong the content is. Switching to a static HTML export, a Next.js server-side render, or even a simple HTML fallback for the core content fixes the reachability problem at the crawl layer. (2) Add FAQPage JSON-LD schema to the portfolio page, wrapping the two or three questions a recruiter most often asks (e.g., "What type of design work does she do?" / "What projects has she shipped?") with 80-word direct answers carrying specific project names, outcomes, and dates. The mechanism: FAQPage pre-shapes the answer into the format an AI engine's retrieval step wants to lift into a synthesized reply, and the first-80-words discipline satisfies the satisficing bias because the engine and the recruiter both take the first plausible answer they encounter. A named credential and a dated case study also carry E-E-A-T signals (Experience and Expertise) that the engine reads as authority signals when deciding whose passage to trust.

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.2, LO 18.3, LO 18.7

Tags: GEO personal brand; FAQPage; client-rendered; E-E-A-T; AACSB: Application of knowledge

SA-4

A mid-size apparel brand has operated since 2019 on a first-party data strategy: it collects email at checkout, hashes and uploads lists to Meta Customer Lists and Google Customer Match, and fires server-side conversion events through the Conversions API. A newly hired performance marketer argues the company should rebuild around third-party cookies now that Google has reversed the deprecation. Use the chapter's cookie-reversal analysis to assess her recommendation, and explain why the brand's existing stack is or is not durable.

Sample answer: The recommendation is based on a misreading of the situation. Google's reversal of third-party-cookie deprecation on July 22, 2024, and its cancellation of the Privacy Sandbox on October 7, 2025, did not restore a stable tracking environment because the cookie was never the only constraint. Safari blocked third-party cookies in 2017 through Intelligent Tracking Prevention, and Firefox reached the same posture in 2019 through Enhanced Tracking Protection. Roughly a third of US traffic was already effectively cookieless before Google's 2020 announcement or its 2024 reversal. The brand's first-party stack, built on hashed emails, server-to-server conversion events, and platform Customer Lists, survives Safari and Firefox traffic regardless of what Chrome does. A brand that rebuilds around third-party cookies is building on a surface that iOS Safari already removed from most mobile traffic and that could disappear again if Google changes direction or if regulators impose new constraints. The chapter's ruling is that the first-party investment is durable whatever Google does next, because the durable surface is the brand-owned identifier, not the platform's cross-site tracker.

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.5

Tags: cookie reversal; first-party data; ATT; Safari ITP; AACSB: Reflective thinking; Application of knowledge

AI-1 — Evaluate an AI-Generated GEO Audit

A student asks Claude: "How do I get my brand cited in ChatGPT answers?" Claude responds:

"To improve your AI citations, focus on creating high-quality content and building backlinks from authoritative sites. Make sure your website loads quickly and is mobile-friendly. Publish blog posts regularly and promote them on social media. Consider running paid ads to increase brand awareness so more people search for your brand by name."

Using the six GEO levers from our chapter, evaluate which, if any, of the AI's recommendations are actually on target, identify the single most important lever the response omits, and explain why the omitted lever has more empirical support than the recommendations given.

A) The AI's response is comprehensive and actionable; the student should implement all five recommendations immediately. B) The AI's recommendations are mostly classical SEO tactics (backlinks, load speed, mobile) that have limited or no documented effect on AI-engine citation share; the most important omitted lever is inline expert quotation, which a 10,000-query benchmark measured at a 41 percent citation lift, the largest of nine levers tested. C) The AI's recommendation to run paid ads is the most important GEO lever because ad spend increases branded search volume, which the engine interprets as authority. D) The AI correctly identifies blog publishing as the primary GEO driver; the student should double the posting frequency. E) The AI's output is correct for Google classical search but needs to be re-run on a different model for AI-engine-specific advice.

Answer: B

Difficulty: 3

Objective: LO 18.3 — The vague question produced generic SEO advice. Backlinks and load speed are classical SEO factors with no documented GEO effect. Paid ads do not influence the retrieval index AI engines use. Blog publishing drives 6 percent of AI citations, not the dominant channel. Inline expert quotation is the chapter's highest-documented single lever (41 percent in the Aggarwal benchmark), and FAQPage schema with 80-word direct answers is the structural complement. The item teaches students to compare vague AI output against the chapter's evidence base.

Tags: GEO audit; AI output evaluation; expert quotation; FAQPage; AACSB: Reflective thinking; Application of knowledge

AI-2 — Generate and Evaluate FAQPage JSON-LD

A student uses the chapter's FAQPage JSON-LD generator widget for a running-shoe retailer. The widget returns the following markup:

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "What running shoes are best?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "It depends on many factors. There are lots of great options available in different price ranges from many brands."
      }
    }
  ]
}

The student plans to paste this into her site's HTML and considers it done. Using the chapter's GEO criteria for effective FAQPage schema, identify the two most critical problems with this markup and explain what a corrected version should include.

Sample answer: Problem 1 — The answer violates the 80-word direct-answer principle. The response "It depends on many factors... lots of great options" is vague and not extractable. An AI engine's retrieval step wants to lift a passage that directly answers the question, and a buyer who satisfices on the first plausible answer will not find a plausible answer here. The corrected answer should open with the direct recommendation, for example naming the specific conditions (flat feet, high arch, trail vs. road, under $150) the retailer's top seller is designed for, with the specific product and key specifications stated in the first sentence, so the engine's lift step has something concrete to attribute to the retailer. Problem 2 — The question "What running shoes are best?" is a head-term query too broad for the engine to retrieve this retailer's passage on, because hundreds of more authoritative sources (Runner's World, REI, major brands) own that query. Effective FAQPage questions target the specific sub-queries a buyer of this retailer's size and inventory could plausibly win: "What running shoes are best for flat feet under $150?" or "Which trail running shoes have a wide toe box?" so the passage competes in a retrievable pool rather than against the entire category. The corrected markup should wrap each specific sub-query the retailer can back with real product-level expertise and a named 80-word answer that opens with the direct response.

Difficulty: 3

Objective: LO 18.7, LO 18.3 — The item requires students to apply the 80-word direct-answer rule and the sub-query specificity principle to evaluate and improve generated markup, moving from auditing to shipping as the Apply box instructs.

Tags: FAQPage JSON-LD; direct answer; sub-query; GEO; AACSB: Application of knowledge; Written communication

AI-3 — Diagnose an Agentic Commerce Readiness Gap

A cosmetics brand's marketing team reads about ChatGPT Instant Checkout and the Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP). They check their brand's status and find the following: the brand appears in ChatGPT answers when buyers ask for recommendations in its category, the brand's product pages are fully server-side rendered in plain HTML, and the brand processes payments through Stripe. However, the brand has no structured Product Feed Spec file published for the AI agent to read.

Which of the following correctly describes the brand's current competitive position in agentic commerce, and what is the single highest-priority action?

A) The brand is fully ACP-ready because its product pages are in plain HTML and Stripe is its processor; no additional action is required. B) The brand is cited in the AI answer but is not a buyable option inside the chat, because the agent cannot read product details, live pricing, or inventory without a structured Product Feed Spec; publishing the feed is the highest-priority action since Stripe integration reportedly requires approximately one additional line of code for a brand already processing through Stripe. C) The brand should switch from Stripe to another payment processor, because only non-Stripe merchants can participate in ChatGPT Instant Checkout. D) The brand is at a disadvantage because its pages are in plain HTML rather than a JavaScript framework, and ACP requires JavaScript rendering to function. E) The brand should pause GEO work and focus exclusively on ACP setup, since citation share has no effect on agentic purchase likelihood.

Answer: B

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.6 — The chapter states the Product Feed Spec is the commerce twin of FAQPage schema: GEO gets the brand cited, and ACP lets the cited brand be purchased. A brand cited but absent from the product feed "gets talked about while a competitor gets bought." The Stripe integration note is from the chapter: a Stripe processor is one line of code from enabling agentic payments. Plain HTML is the correct reachability posture, not a disadvantage.

Tags: ACP; Product Feed Spec; ChatGPT Instant Checkout; Stripe; AACSB: Application of knowledge

AI-4 — Evaluate the Four-Tier AI Automation Ladder

A brand manager is deciding how much to invest in automating her weekly GEO citation-share audit. She currently runs the audit manually each Monday by asking ChatGPT about her category and writing down which brands are cited. She is considering moving to one of the three higher tiers the chapter describes. Her constraints: she spends about 45 minutes on the audit each week, she has access to the brand's Search Console data and a Slack channel the team monitors, and the team's monthly digital-marketing software budget is $200.

Which tier should she move to next, and what does it add relative to her current manual approach?

A) She should stay at Tier 1 (chat with web search) because her current approach is equivalent and no investment is justified. B) She should move to Tier 2 (a saved Skill like /geo-audit) which removes the need to retype the prompt each week, running the same audit on command for roughly 30 to 60 cents per run, with no new software subscription required. C) She should skip to Tier 4 immediately (scheduled agent posting to Slack) because her Slack and Search Console access makes it the most efficient choice regardless of the setup complexity. D) She should move to Tier 3 (MCP server connecting Search Console and Reddit live) because the $200 budget comfortably funds the $30 a month scheduled-agent option at Tier 4 and she should maximize automation. E) She should switch to a vendor monitoring platform at $400 per month to ensure enterprise-grade citation tracking.

Answer: B

Difficulty: 2

Objective: LO 18.3 / course AI-integration — The chapter's instruction is to move up one tier only when the last one becomes a chore, and Tier 2 is the natural next step for her: it eliminates the 45-minute prompt-rewriting routine by saving the procedure as a slash-command Skill, runs for cents per use, and requires no monthly software spend. Tier 4 fits her Slack and Search Console access, but the chapter advises against skipping tiers. Tier 3 at $30/month fits her budget, making it a reasonable future step, but Tier 2 is the correct next move, not an immediate jump to automation.

Tags: AI audit tiers; Skill; MCP; GEO monitoring; automation ladder; AACSB: Application of knowledge; Analytical thinking
