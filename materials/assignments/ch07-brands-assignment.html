Graded individual assignment for Chapter 7 (Brands & Brand Equity), mapped to the chapter's learning objectives and AACSB outcomes.

Build a Brand Analysis App with Claude

Chapter 7 individual assignment. Pick a real brand, have Claude gather what the market actually thinks of it, and then direct Claude to build you an interactive app that maps the brand against its rivals. You will not write code. You design the analysis, judge what the model pulled, and read the positioning off a tool you built in an afternoon. Done alone.

Chapter: 7 — Brands &amp; Brand Equity &middot; Type: Individual (graded, solo) &middot; Time: about 2 hours &middot; You will need: a Claude account (claude.ai) with Artifacts.

Pick the brand

One real brand you can find public information on, with two or three nameable competitors: a DTC brand, a campus or local business, or a brand you actually use. Public information only.

Build the analysis with Claude

Work in claude.ai. You direct, Claude builds. Three moves:

  1. Gather the evidence. Ask Claude to web-search the brand and its rivals and pull what the market thinks: how each one positions itself, what reviews and social posts praise and complain about, and the two or three attributes buyers actually compare on in this category (price, quality, design, sustainability, status, whatever fits). Every claim it returns is a claim to verify against the real site or reviews.
  1. Build the app. Tell Claude to make an interactive Artifact: a perceptual map (the Chapter 7 tool) that plots your brand and its rivals on the two attributes that matter most, with each brand clickable to show its points of parity, its points of difference, and one reason to believe. Iterate with Claude until the map is honest and readable. You are building a real, shareable tool, not a static chart.
  1. Read the positioning. Use the app you built. Where does your brand sit? Where is the open space, the position no rival owns? State the brand's current position in one line, and the move you would make: hold it, sharpen it, or reposition onto a different attribute.

The graded core: out-judge what Claude built

Claude gathers and plots all of this fast, and some of it will be wrong. Most of your grade is the judgment pass. Write up, in your own words:

  • One axis Claude chose that does not actually drive the category, and the one that does. A perceptual map is only as good as its two axes.
  • One brand it mis-placed, or one "fact" it invented: a review theme that is not real, a competitor that does not compete, a feature the brand does not have. Name it, check it against the real source, and fix it.
  • Where the map is right on the dots but silent on the story: what the positioning actually means for the brand, which a scatter plot cannot tell you.

Then make your positioning call. The app is Claude's build; the brand judgment is yours.

Hand in

  1. The working Artifact: paste the published share link, or attach a short screen recording of you using it.
  2. A one-page brand read: the brand's current position, the open space on the map, your hold / sharpen / reposition call and the attribute it turns on, and the brand's points of parity, points of difference, and one reason to believe.
  3. The AI transcript and your judgment pass: the prompts you gave Claude, and the axis, mis-placement, or invented fact you caught and corrected. This is your disclosure, and it is graded.
