Analyzing Cost and Price Structures with Claude in Excel

Chapter 10 individual assignment. You are the new analyst at a direct-to-consumer brand, handed a spreadsheet of its product line. Use the Claude for Excel add-in to turn raw operating data into a cost-and-price-structure analysis: which products actually make money, where each one breaks even, and which price should change. Then do the part the add-in cannot: catch what it got wrong and decide what to recommend. Done alone.

Chapter: 10 — Pricing &middot; Type: Individual (graded, solo) &middot; Time: about 2 hours &middot; You will need: Excel (desktop or web) and the dataset below.

The dataset

Download trailhead-supply-product-line.csv: one quarter of operating data for Trailhead Supply Co., a DTC outdoor-gear brand. Twelve SKUs, each with units sold, unit price, unit variable cost, stock on hand, and returns. The company's fixed cost is $128,000 per quarter (salaries, rent, software, and overhead, not tied to any single product). Open the file in Excel.

Set up Claude for Excel

Install the add-in: in Excel, open Home → Add-ins → More Add-ins, search "Claude by Anthropic," click Add, and sign in with your Claude account. The sidebar opens on the right (shortcut Ctrl+Alt+C on Windows, Ctrl+Option+C on Mac). It reads your open workbook automatically, writes real formulas into cells, and cites the cells it used. Claude for Excel runs on a paid Claude plan (Pro, Max, Team, or Enterprise).

No paid plan? Upload the CSV to claude.ai or ChatGPT and have it run the same analysis in its data-analysis (code) sandbox. The work and the judgment are identical; you only lose the live in-sheet editing.

Run the analysis with Claude

Direct the assistant through the chapter's cost arithmetic, and make it write the formulas in the sheet so every number traces to a cell:

  1. Contribution margin per SKU (unit price minus unit variable cost) and the contribution-margin ratio. Sort the products by it.
  2. Breakeven. Each product's breakeven volume, and the line's overall breakeven against the $128,000 fixed cost. Which SKUs sold above breakeven last quarter, and which did not?
  3. Markup versus margin. A column for each on every SKU; confirm they are not the same number. Markup is on cost and can pass 100%; margin is on price and never can.
  4. One pricing scenario. Pick a product, have Claude model a price change (a raise or a cut), and show the new contribution margin and the new breakeven volume. Is the change worth it?
  5. A chart or pivot that makes the structure visible: contribution by SKU, or margin against volume.

The graded core: out-judge the analysis

Claude produces all of that in minutes, confidently, and some of it will be wrong or beside the point. Your real work, and most of the grade, is the judgment pass. Find and write up, in your own words:

  • A product the model treats as healthy that is not. One SKU in this file loses money on every unit it sells. Name it, show the arithmetic, and say what you would do.
  • An item that does not belong in a product breakeven at all, and why including it distorts the line.
  • One formula or framing error you caught and fixed: a markup reported as a margin, the fixed cost spread the wrong way, a breakeven that ignored returns, a cell the model misread.
  • The call Claude will not make for you: the single price or product you would change first, and the number to move it to.

Then give the founder three recommendations. The analysis is Claude's; the judgment and the call are yours.

Hand in

One PDF plus the workbook:

  1. The finished Excel file (or a screenshot of the analysis sheet with the formulas visible).
  2. A one-page memo to the founder: the products that make the most and least sense, the loss-making SKU and your fix, and your highest-priority price change with its new breakeven.
  3. The AI transcript and your judgment pass: the prompts you gave Claude and the errors or blind spots you caught and corrected. This is your disclosure, and it is graded.
